I've recently been traveling by air solo more than I was before to go to competitions and camps, et cetera. The issue with traveling by air is, of course, that you'll often have to pay more fees if flying on a budget carrier should you bring a checked bag; hell, even some decent carriers like Southwest (well, they're not as good as they were before for sure...) charge for first checked bags nowadays. That's led to the idea of one-bag travel becoming more and more popular over time. There is one issue with the space, though; every single guide you will find on YouTube or anything else, bar none, serves to advertise whatever bags or travel organizers or, god forbid, merino wool clothing they're shoehorning into the video. Therefore, as someone who's both not financially independent and also flies solo with one bag, I think my perspective might be useful! 

## What to pack
One-bag travel can be shoehorned into any backpack, of course. However, there are some types of bags that are better than others. If you pack extremely light, then you can forgo getting a different bag altogether and just use whatever backpack you have. This is ideal but may not be an option for some... myself included.

Travel organizers are total scams. As someone who's got one of those crazy fancy Cordura X-Pak whatever hanging dopp kits... it's really not that deep. You can buy perfectly adequate travel organizers at like Ross for less than $10 each and they'll do great. Hell, Ziploc bags will suffice. Don't be lured by marketing unless it's really something you want (probably isn't).

Pack only the tech that you actually need. There's no point carrying a phone, a tablet, a laptop, a Switch or whatever, individual charging bricks for each... whatever. If you really need them then sure, but if you can do without, do without. I pack my phone, my laptop, and my laptop charigng brick because they're both USB-C, and that's all. 

Clothes are the single biggest hoarder of space in any baggage in 99% of cases, so you'll want to reduce the amount you pack, obviously. You should also consider what is both easy to wash, dries fast, and doesn't smell shit. The best way to pack light with these considerations in mind is to pack some detergent and wash your clothes wherever you've traveled to. There is not a place on earth that doesn't have facilities for you to wash your clothes in some way. The way that works best for me without spending any money at all is as follows, takes a little time before you shower:

- Firstly, add the dirty clothes to a large and nonporous plastic bag. I personally find doing laundry directly in the sink nasty, but I mean... whatever floats your boat, ig
- You obviously add soap. I bring laundry detergent sheets since they're easy to pack and doesn't face any fluid restrictions. There's also no risk of it leaking. You could use hotel shampoo if you're desperate.
- Add sink water at whatever temperature is appropriate, agitate the soap, and let it soak for a few minutes. I wait like 15 minutes usually.
- When you go to take your shower, dump the clothes out the bag and rinse them well + remember to wring them out
- Once you're done drying off after showering, roll the clothes up in another towel and squeeze. 
- If your hotel has a clothesline, great. I personally pack a travel one, it was literally $5 and it works great. Having a fan on will speed drying time a lot.

If you're able and willing to do laundry whereever you've gone, you wlil save SO much space because you can pack so much less. 

This now brings us to the problem of what type of clothes to pack. The considerations that you'll want to make here are that certain fabrics dry faster than others, have different levels of stank they get up to, etc. Generally, I would say get stuff that's of decently good quality synthetic fabric, ie. stuff from Uniqlo or something similar. Cotton is generally the worst option since it both takes forever to dry and also wrinkles incredibly fast if packed tightly... obviously, if that's your only option, then just pack whatever. You can wear pants for a few days without washing them and it won't get too bad, so you can go light on that front. The biggest thing is don't pack more than you need, and make sure to do your laundry. If you don't want to do your laundry at your accomodations, then just forget this whole one bag thing. (unless it's like a half week lol)

Toiletry wise... All I would personally say is to separate the stuff for your body as opposed to your teeth because if your face wash or something leaks onto your toothbrush, basically you've got no option but to throw that shit away.

Now, I'm personally fine using whatever body wash is at the accomodations; I cannot use the shampoo or conidtioner, so I fill some travel bottles with my shampoo, conditioner, curl cream, and mousse. If this doesn't apply to you, just bring whatever you need. Remember what I said earlier about separating the stuff for your teeth vs everything else, though.

## My (PERSONAL) packing list

It may surprise you but I do actually only pack a single carry on bag for everything and a little personal item (a small crossbody). I've done a few trips like this and each time I didn't feel as though I sacrificed much by packing as light as I did. 
I don't have a fancy clamshell backpack, I use a basic Carhartt 25L backpack. There's not much to write home about other than that its material's decently robust and there's just one main compartment, so there's a lot of flexibility in packing. This is crucial for one-bag travel.

Here's the main things to note. I don't really need cold-weather clothes since most of the places I go to where I need to travel with one bag either aren't cold or I'll be inside most of the time anyways:
- 3 sets of t-shirts, socks, and underwear. I'll wash these often. If you include what I wear to the airport, there's 4 pairs.
- 1 pair of loose jeans for casual wear, and if necessary, maybe a pair of khakis. I wear a pair of khaki dad shorts to the flight.
- I wear a button down shirt to the airport, doubles as a sort of light coat I guess. and I can use it if I need a formal-ish outfit.
- A pair of sandals because I'm asian. I wear my sneakers to the flight and they look put-together enough that I can get away with wearing them with business casual.
- A microfiber towel for my hair
- Prior mentioned stuff for laundry...

That's all the clothes I bring, since as I mentioned you can wash your laundry anywhere. This all goes into a zippable clothes bag for organization. I also pack my mini clothesline in the same bag.

### Other obligatory stuff;

- Laptop + its charger (USB-C, so that's the only charger I bring). Mine is a 14.5 inch workstation laptop (Thinkpad P14s) so it's pretty heavy and thicker than most laptops. That being said, it's not too bad since I don't need a separate charger that takes up an awkward shape for this one.
- Water bottle + my chopsticks and spork
- Pen case, I also pack an ink vial
- Little pack of tissues
- My toothbrush + toothpaste and floss
- Shaving razor + blades
- Face wash + moisturizer, sunscreen, and toner

Now, I mentioned before I don't need to pack body wash and stuff. The same does not apply to hair products since my hair type happens to be really wavy and I put a decent amount of effort into that shit. Therefore, most the remainder ends up being taken up by haircare products. These are all in travel containers that I fill beforehand.
- Shampoo + conditioner (if you're wondering, all my stuff is Shea Moisture brand)
- Curl cream
- Mousse
- Leave-in conditioner
- Dry shampoo (Kitsch brand)
- Diffuser that I can just clip onto any hair dryer

Other than that, eveyrthing else is in my pockets.
